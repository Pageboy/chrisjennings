Cruising Alone

13/8/2010

 
Time to take off on my own for a trip upstream. A bit nervous about handling the ropes through the locks but there was no need!

No parking for the car at Bossoms so a bit of a delay until 12.15. Spotted Tarsus (my father’s old boat) on the other side of Godstow lock. Police divers at Godstow bridge - thought it might be a body but lock-keeper informed me that they were simply cleaning up the river. Diver swam to the bank and allowed me to pass under the bridge.

Encountered rather rude (later apologised) narrow boat. They only left one half of the lock gates open and I had to tie up and open up the other half. One gate is not enough for Aviva. Whilst fiddling about in the lock (keeper was at lunch), Environment Agency boat turned up. Pleasant chat with the skipper.

Sunday cruise to Bablock Hythe

8/8/2010

 
A late start for us on a Sunday, never-the-less, we managed to reach Bablock Hythe for Strawberries on the grass. The field at Bablock Hythe is private, but a game of frisbie in bare feet is such a pleasure on grass cropped so short.

The light was beautiful on our return downstream, and since a member of our crew walked from Godstow back to Medley this gave us a rare photo opportunity.

You can see one of these photos below. Only now do I see how the fenders are strung out in such a peculiar (yet practical) arrangement.

New Engine Temperature probe

29/7/2010

 
One of the electrical contacts on the old temperature sender had broken off. This meant no connection to the alarm and light on the panel. A new one was ordered from Beta Marine.

Cooling water was partially drained (and saved) and the old sender came out with no problem. The new one is certainly a different size but fortunately the staff at Beta Marine send an adaptor and this made the fitting straightforward.

Children Love Crayfishing

26/7/2010

 
Our friends from New Zealand - Wilfried, Kaye and children spent the day upstream. We moored up above Kings Lock. A bit of bacon on a piece of string soon results in screams of joy as crayfish are caught and popped in the bucket. We know that these creatures are actually non-native American signal crayfish and they can be trapped commercially and sold to restaurants. Technically, they are pests and we shouldn’t have thrown them back ...

An excursion with Wilfried

22/7/2010

 
Took a friend from New Zealand out on the water. We started out with the covers off. A few showers later and we had the covers back on. Even the windscreen wipers worked. Wilfried commented that these reminded him of Land Rover wipers.

The horses ankle deep at the water’s edge looked picturesque. The grebes and geese scurried away as we passed.

An Evening Cruise

22/6/2010

 
Set off from our mooring at 6.15pm. Tied up above Godstow lock had a supper of chicken soup and lovely bread. Strawberries for pudding. Gave the dishes a first wash in the river. Godstow lock can be frustratingly slow when on automatic. 

Beautiful light as we made our passage across Port Meadow. Back on the mooring at 8.45. Skipper is getting good at squeezing Aviva into the mooring space!

First Swim in the River

23/5/2010

 
Blistering hot day! Trip up to Eynsham. Filled with diesel.

Very busy on Port meadow - on land: loads of picnics, on water: rowers and dinghy sailing. Godstow lock with a keeper not seen before. Smooth run up to Kings. Onwards to Eynsham lock. We always seem to arrive here at lunchtime for a spot of ‘Self service’. We don’t mind this, since this hand operated lock is fun!

Oxford Cruisers had a look at the leaking shaft seal, although wasn’t able to solve there and then. I am not able to go too fast until fixed.

Through Pinkhill Lock and tied up under Farmoor reservoir. We swam. Zoe was first in (brave girl). Skipper went in (underpants). Note to self to get boarding ladder since it was very difficult to get back on the boat.
